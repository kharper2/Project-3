Five Stages of Grief: Music Edition

Denial

When I was four, my mother read that immersing children in music was beneficial with learning math in school.

As a result, I was forced into playing the piano.

I couldn&t believe it! How could my own mother tourture me so?

  • My teacher was mean.
  • I didn&t like the songs I was assigned to practice.
  • I never understood what was so beautiful about pressing keys̵1–it felt impersonal. Or as my child self would say, boring.

It was the worst time of my life

Anger

I had had enough. I grew to resent playing the piano and I quit.

Bargaining

My mother told me I needed to pick up another instrument.

I simply didn&t see the value in doing so.

Depression

I was overwhelmed. I felt helpless.

No other instrument appealed to me, but my mother would not give up.

Acceptance

Then, like a sign from the heavens, I saw my saving grace: That which posses two mustaches and the tail of a croissant.

I was in church when I saw people playing the violin and knew that that was the instrument I wanted to play.
